Frequently asked questions

Senior Exemption

Backers of the El Cerrito Library Tax sold their idea in part by telling folks there is a senior exemption. But that's not really true.

If you read the fine print of the ballot question, you'll see that there's a possible exemption that seniors can apply for. However, they can only apply if they are enrolled in one of two state programs:

    1. Gonzales-Deukmejian-Petris Senior Citizen Property Tax Assistance Law

    2. Senior Citizen Property Tax Postponement Law

What are those two programs, and do they cover all El Cerrito seniors? Not at all.

Program 1: Gonzales-Deukmejian-Petris

This program hasn't been funded by the State of California since 2008 and cannot process claims since there's no funding.

Program 2: Senior Citizen Property Tax Postponement

This program has a maximum household income of ~$55k and does not apply to renters. It includes other requirements, such as 40% home equity, no reverse mortgage on the property, and the state will put a lien on your house. Less than 1% of seniors statewide are enrolled in this program.

From the Ballot Question

Here's the exact text. Notice the long application process:

"Single-family residential parcels and units under separate ownership on multi-family residential parcels shall be exempt from the Special Tax if one of the owners qualifies for participation in either or both the Gonzales-Deukmejian-Petris Senior Citizen Property Tax Assistance Law (also known as the Senior Citizens Homeowners and Renters Property Tax Assistance Law and the Homeowners and Renters Property Tax Assistance Law) or the Senior Citizen Property Tax Postponement Law. Qualifying applicants must occupy the property and be at least partially legally responsible for payment of the ad valorem tax for the property. Applications may require proof that the State Controller's Office or Franchise Tax Board has approved the applicant's participation in either of the programs named in this section. Applications for this exemption must be submitted to the Tax Administrator in the manner and at the time established by the Tax Administrator. Applications shall be on forms provided by the Tax Administrator and shall provide such information as the Tax Administrator may require. If the Tax Administrator determines the need to audit an application, the Tax Administrator may require additional information, including, but not limited to, federal income tax returns and W-2 forms of owner-occupants eligible for this exemption."

Bottom line: Even if a senior is eligible to apply for the exemption, there is no guarantee that the city will approve the application. Don't let the Pro Library Tax advocates fool you or your loved ones!
