El Cerrito Library Tax  •  June 2, 2026

Why Measure C
Is Bad for Business

Libraries are good. Measure C is not.

A new parcel tax — up to $0.17/sq ft — with no exemptions for commercial properties and a 30-year horizon.
How it works

Tax structure

Annual parcel tax on all El Cerrito properties. Rate scales with building square footage — so larger commercial and industrial spaces pay significantly more. The City can increase the rate annually, and the 30-year clock doesn't start until bonds are issued, potentially in the 2030s.

 Who pays

Direct impact on businesses

Owners pay the tax directly. Tenants under NNN or pass-through leases will likely see higher rent or CAM charges. Larger storefronts, offices, warehouses, and retail spaces bear the greatest burden — and there are no exemptions for commercial properties.

Indirect effects

Higher fixed costs push businesses to raise prices or cut staffing and hours. El Cerrito retailers and food-service operators may face a cost disadvantage relative to nearby cities without similar taxes.

No relief available

No commercial exemptions exist. The burden falls across the entire business sector — from national chains to local independents — with larger properties paying disproportionately more over time.
